## Pattern Notes — VSL Structure

**Length spectrum:**
- Chase AI+: **1:08** — punchy, single-purpose, money-shot wins
- Maker School: **12:21** — long-form, multi-section, full sales argument

**Hook openers:**
- Maker School: personal intro + immediate outcome promise + immediate guarantee ("get customer #1 in 90 days, or I refund you")
- Chase AI+: outcome-led ("If you're ready to master Claude Code, you've come to the right place")

**Core elements present in both:**
1. Outcome promise upfront
2. Guarantee / risk reversal
3. Social proof — named student wins with $ amounts (both list multiple, Maker rapid-fires 15+, Chase shows 3)
4. Authority / why-me ("I've grown two agencies to $92k/month" / "the same tool I use")
5. Direct CTA at the end

**Maker School's longer structure (for reference if going long):**
1. Hook (0:00) — what is it + outcome + guarantee
2. Social proof barrage (0:27)
3. Why qualified (2:17)
4. Who it's for (2:52)
5. How it works — the 4 things (3:28)
6. What's included (6:53)
7. Platform tour (8:37)
8. Pricing structure (9:53)
9. ROI math (10:48)
10. Objection handling — "if you're on the fence" (11:16)
11. Soft close (12:06)

**Tactical lessons for our re-record:**
- Lead with the outcome + guarantee in the first 10 seconds
- Use named, specific student wins ($X amount, Y timeframe) — vague "people are getting results" doesn't sell
- Show why you specifically can teach this (not just credentials, but the specific receipts)
- Direct CTA at the end — "I'll see you inside"
- Length depends on price point: Chase at $98 = 1 minute. Nick at $184 = 12 minutes. Our $75–97 should likely sit closer to Chase's length than Nick's.
