## Pattern Notes (for Backbone work)

**Outcome promise pattern:**
- Maker School: *"Land your first paying client in 90 days, or money back"*
- Chase AI+: *"90 day guide... land your first client, or your money back"*
- AIS+: No explicit outcome guarantee on about page — has "100% Resolution Guarantee" on tech support only, links to separate refund policy doc

**Common deliverable categories:**
1. Big video library / day-by-day roadmap (Maker: 218 videos, Chase: Masterclass + Playbook)
2. Templates / agents / pre-built assets (Maker: 40+ scripts, Chase: 100+ templates, AIS+: hands-on projects)
3. Live coaching / Q&A (Maker: daily + weekly with Nick, Chase implied, AIS+: weekly recorded Q&A)
4. Community + networking ("over 2,000 other AI consultants", job opportunities)
5. Software / discount stack (Maker: $21K in software discounts is a unique angle)
6. Bonuses (gift-stack format with $ values attached)

**Pricing/urgency tactics:**
- Maker: "Currently $184, soon $204" — implied price increase
- Chase: "40% flash sale ends 5/3" + "Price increase at 1.1K members" + "Lock in $58/mo annual at checkout"
- AIS+: No urgency language — relies on the tier's 3.6K member proof

**Hook-style openers (first line of about):**
- Maker: claim of being fastest + most affordable + Guaranteed
- Chase: 6-word headline ("Learn AI. Land Clients.") + flash-sale alert
- AIS+: personal credibility hook ("I built an agency from zero to $1M/yr")
- AIS free: third-person credibility ("verified n8n Expert Partner with 650K YT subs")

**Refund policy:**
- Maker: simple "or your money back"
- Chase: "or your money back" baked into the 90-day-guide bullet
- AIS+: links out to separate refund policy doc (ImplementsThe stipulations approach we discussed)
