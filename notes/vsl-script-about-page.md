## The Script

> **[0:00 — HOOK / OUTCOME PROMISE]**
>
> Hey, I'm Jason. Inside Creator Accelerator, I do two things. If you're just getting started as a creator, I'll help you ship your first viral video. If you're already running a business and posting consistently, I'll help you 10x your views using AI content systems. In the next two minutes, I'll walk you through exactly how it works — and exactly who's doing it right now.
>
> **[0:20 — REFRAME OF FAILURE]**
>
> Here's the thing nobody is going to tell you. The reason most creators never hit either of those outcomes isn't talent, isn't the algorithm, isn't your camera. It's that you're building every single piece of content from scratch. Every video, every hook, every script — blank page, white knuckles, willpower. That works for a couple weeks. Then you burn out and ghost. The problem isn't *you*. It's that you don't have a system.
>
> **[0:40 — DIFFERENTIATOR]**
>
> Every "AI program" on the internet right now is teaching you to sell AI services to businesses. That's not what we do. Inside Creator Accelerator, you're not building an agency — you're building a content engine. An AI system that takes *your* voice, *your* niche, *your* best ideas, and turns them into videos that actually hit. Every week. Without you grinding from zero each time.
>
> **[1:03 — MECHANISM]**
>
> Here's how it works. You get my plug-and-play viral content system — the actual files, the prompts, the templates I use to ship every video. Two coaching calls a week where I personally review your content and tell you what's wrong. Custom AI research built around your niche so you're never guessing what to post. And the full 2026 content course that walks you through every piece of it. Same system running behind the scenes for me and 173 other creators inside the community right now.
>
> **[1:35 — PROOF — MAPPED TO BOTH OUTCOMES]**
>
> Take Albert. Came in stuck at 200 views per video — couldn't crack his first viral. Seven weeks later: three viral hits, averaging 5 to 10 thousand views, and he grew his own paid community from 5 members to 19. That's outcome one. Then there's Ayden — already running his business, doing $2–3K a month. Six months on the system, he's at $10K a month. That's outcome two. These aren't outliers. They're the people who installed the system and actually ran it.
>
> **[2:05 — ANTI-LARP POSITIONING]**
>
> I'll be straight with you. I dropped $30,000 on guru programs before I figured any of this out. Most of them were overpriced LARP. So I refuse to charge $10,000 for what should cost $75. You shouldn't have to mortgage your future to learn how to grow online.
>
> **[2:25 — OFFER + REAL URGENCY]**
>
> Right now Creator Accelerator is $75 a month. On May 18 it flips to $97 — but anyone who joins before then is locked in at $75 for life. If you're already monetizing and you want done-for-you, the $4,000 Inner Circle includes a 90-day install-monetize-scale plan, an editor placed on your team at month three, and a 10x-views-in-90-days guarantee. Five spots a month.
>
> **[2:50 — SOFT CLOSE]**
>
> If you've made it this far, you already know whether this is for you. If it is — I'll see you inside. If not, no hard feelings, keep shipping. Either way, I appreciate you.

## Why this structure (the persuasion logic)

| Beat | Why it's there | Lifted from |
|------|----------------|-------------|
| Outcome-led hook (split for both ICP halves) | All three comps lead with outcome. Splitting it ("first viral" / "10x your views") qualifies both buyer types in 20 seconds without a separate "who it's for" section. | Nick + Chase + Nate all lead outcome-first |
| Reframe of failure (system, not skill) | Move the buyer from "I'm not good enough" → "I just don't have this thing yet." Makes the product the obvious fix. Now anchored to the two outcomes promised in the hook. | Nate's "you don't have clients because you have nothing to show" |
| Differentiator before features | The unique-angle moat is *creators using AI to make content*, not *selling AI to businesses*. Buyer needs this frame before features land. | Original to Jason's positioning |
| Mechanism named concretely | Files, prompts, calls, course — specific deliverables, not abstract "transformation" | Maker School's "200 day-by-day modules" specificity |
| Proof = 2 named wins, mapped to the 2 outcomes | Albert = outcome 1 (first viral), Ayden = outcome 2 (10x revenue from monetized base). Mirrors the hook structure end-to-end. | Maker School's named-win cadence + outcome-mapping logic |
| Anti-LARP beat = vulnerability + status | "$30K on guru programs" lowers status (relatable), "I refuse to charge $10K for what should cost $75" raises it (operator stance) | Maker's bankrupt-childhood beat, repurposed |
| Real urgency (May 17 flip) | Not manufactured scarcity. Grandfathered-for-life pricing is the strongest lever Jason has right now | Original to Jason's offer |
| Premium upsell tucked in late | Nate's permission-framed annual upsell move — only the qualified self-select | Nate's annual upsell architecture |
| Anti-pitch close | "Either way, I appreciate you" = abundance signal. Higher status than begging for the sale. | Nick's "even traffic counts as a win" |

## Production notes

- **Camera:** Talking head, eye-level, well-lit. Skool VSLs that out-convert tend to feel personal-not-polished — don't over-produce.
- **Pacing:** Read at natural pace, not "VSL voice." Jason's brand is operator-not-influencer; performative urgency would break it.
- **B-roll/cutaways:** Optional but powerful at three points: (1) the AI content system files when you say "the actual files, prompts, templates" (0:58), (2) Albert's analytics screen at "three viral hits" (1:30), (3) the Skool community feed at "173 other creators" (1:25).
- **Captions:** Add captions. ~50% of viewers watch muted on mobile. Names + dollar figures need to be readable.
- **Hook A/B:** The hook is the highest-leverage line. All three alternates stay outcome-led (matching what works in Nick / Chase / Nate). Test these if the primary under-performs:
  - *"Hey, I'm Jason. Two outcomes inside this community: your first viral video, or 10x your existing views using AI content systems. Either of those sound like what you need? Here's how it works."*
  - *"Hey, I'm Jason. Inside Creator Accelerator, beginners ship their first viral video, and creators already running a business 10x their views with AI. In two minutes I'll show you exactly how — and the members doing it right now."*
  - *"Two things happen inside Creator Accelerator. New creators get their first viral video. Established creators 10x what they're already getting. The system that does both is what I'm about to walk you through."*

## Things to confirm before recording

1. **Ayden's $2–3K → $10K stat.** He's flagged as a soft-churn risk in memory (wants to switch offers, skipped a group call). The win itself is real and earned. Decision: still use it, or substitute? If substituting, who's the third win we can name?
2. **"173 members"** — confirm the live count when recording (it'll likely be different by the time the VSL ships).
3. **Price flip date.** Script says May 18. After May 18, the urgency hook needs to be re-recorded — either (a) update to a new flip ($97 → $X on date Y), or (b) replace with a different urgency lever (5-spot Inner Circle cap, cohort start date, etc).
4. **Inner Circle availability.** "Five spots a month" is the offer doc spec — confirm spots are actually open before this goes live, or pull the line.

## What I deliberately did NOT do

- **No 12-min length.** $75 + warm YouTube traffic ≠ Maker School's situation. Long VSLs at this price point give over-thinkers room to talk themselves out.
- **No "without quitting your day job"** language. Most creators *are* the job. Replaced with the post-ghost-cycle pain point, which lands harder.
- **No "first paying client" guarantee** at the Standard tier. Creator outcomes aren't binary like agency outcomes. The 10x-views-in-90-days guarantee lives at the Premium tier where it's actually defensible.
- **No drip-feed gating** of modules. At $75 against creators trained on instant access, gating reads as cheap, not premium.
- **No ROI math.** Creators buy on aspiration and identity, not spreadsheets. The Premium tier alone justifies any ROI conversation; the Standard tier sells on feel.
